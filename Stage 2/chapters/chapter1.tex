\chapter{Introduction}

In the rapid development of the information era, many daily events are achieved by a various of electronic devices such as computer or phones.
Those electronic devices highly rely on integrated circuits(ICs) to handle those events. For example, devices 
might contain personal data, and many events including usage of sensitive information like bank transactions. Therefore, information security, especially authentication 
has become important on peoples' devices to protect from confidential data. However, this required devices to handle challenging problems like energy consumption, 
computational power and the ability to fight against cyber attack. \par

The traditional method that stores and manage secret in memory is expensive and vulnerable to invasion attack. PUF(physical unclonable function) has the ability to deal with these challenges. 
According to the random variation that is inevitable in ICs manufacturing process, PUF make use of the fact that no two IC have exact same physical characteristic. 
This property is the critical idea of PUF.
